Tokiwa Mountain

Poem #148

思ひいづるときはの山の郭公唐紅のふりいでてぞなく
omoiizuru
tokiwa no yama no
hototogisu
karakurenai no
furiidete zo naku
When memories press
I lose myself in weeping,
noisy as the voice
of the crimson-mouthed cuckoo
crying on Evergreen Hill

Poem #251

紅葉せぬときはの山は吹く風のおとにや秋をききわたるらむ
momiji senu
tokiwa no yama wa
fuku kaze no
oto ni ya aki o
kikiwataruramu
Evergreen Mountain,
stranger to seasonal hues -
we wonder if now
it might be hearing autumn
in the blowing of the wind.
"tokiwa is possibly the name of an obscure location, but it also means 'evergreen,'' which along with sakaki (a species of evergreen sacred to kami worship), describe the evergreen leaves that make up the physical offering and the wish for good fortune for ten thousand ages" (Olinyk).

As I could not find any resources pertaining to the location of tokiwa mountain, I borrowed the words of Christina Olinyk. The Kokinshu seems to have most of its utamakura based in the Kinai district, so I would guess that the Tokiwa Mountains would be in the Yamato or Yamashiro province. More importantly, the use of tokiwa here was to contrast the vivid green color with the coming of Autumn and to complain that Autumn hasn't reached the Evergreen hills yet. Complaining of the seasons not matching up with the calendar is common in the Kokinwakashu.

Background Information

Conjures an image of a mountain in autumn colors (Kaori).
