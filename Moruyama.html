Moruyama

Poem #260

しらつゆも時雨もいたくもる山はしたばのこらず色づきにけり
shiratsuyu mo
shigure mo itaku
moruyama wa
shitaba nokorazu
irozukinikeri
The last lower leaf
has been painted with color
at Moruyama,
where white dewdrops and showers
drip onto all the branches.

Background Information

There is a pun on the place name "Moriyama" and "moru" ("leak"; translated "drip") (McCullough).

Moriyama is a posting station in О̄mi (Miner et al.).

Moruyama is also used after the Kokinwakashu in a different fashion, as the "moru" part is part of the verb "mamoru" (to guard, to protect). There is done using a device called kakekotoba (Kamens). For more poems about Moruyama, see Morimoto, Utamakura taikan (it's in Japanese), 373–77.
