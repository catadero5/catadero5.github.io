Fukiage

Poem #272

秋風の吹きあげにたてる白菊は花かあらぬか浪のよするか
akikaze no
fukiage ni tateru
shiragiku wa
hana ka aranu ka
nami no yosuru ka
White chrysanthemums
growing at Fukiage
where autumn winds blow:
are they in truth flowers,
or might waves be rolling in?

Alternate translation (Kaori):
Chrysanthemums at Fukiage Beach in the province of Ki
Are the white chrysanthemums of Fukiage, where autumn winds blow,
Flowers or not? Or perhaps the lapping waves?

Background Information

The background behind poem 272 is interesting: there was a poetic matching game (mono-awase) held by Emperor Uda in 890. In this competition, chrysanthemums were planted in containers with different landscapes and poets had to match the landscape with a placename and tie a poem to the chrysanthemum. Fukiage Beach was associated with rough waves brought about by strong winds. These white chrysanthemums were imagined to be the white sea foam of Fukiage Beach (Kaori). Even though there was no autumn wind at the time, the poet still was able to conjure up the idea of strong autumn winds thanks to its association with Fukiage Beach.
